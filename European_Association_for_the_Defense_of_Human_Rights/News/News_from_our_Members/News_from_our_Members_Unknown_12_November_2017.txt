The Hellenic League for the Defence of Human Rights – Greek criminal justice and prison system again under the radar screen
12 November 2017
This post is also available in: 
    Français (French) Issues related to the administration of criminal justice and the penitentiary system made their return in the public debate. The Hellenic league reminds that this debate cannot only take the form of a discussion between experts, since it concerns the society as a whole. The Hellenic League has repeatedly highlighted the structural problems of both judicial and penitentiary systems : backlogs in cases, extremely high penalties, the imposition of a life sentence to a degree that is appropriate for a third world country, overcrowding in prisons, poor detention conditions, corruption, non-application of alternative detention measures and many others.
