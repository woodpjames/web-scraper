The Hungarian Helsinki Committee analyses and denounces Hungarian asylum legislation’s incompatibility with EU law
29 July 2016
This post is also available in: 
    Français (French) 14 July 2016 – On 5 July 2016, the Hungarian asylum legislation has been subjected to several amendments enabling the police forces to automatically reject asylum seekers arrested next to the Serbian and Croatian borders outside of the country. In its analysis, the Committee considers that this measure violates EU and international law because it prevents them from applying for asylum and lacks fundamental procedural guarantees such as the access to an interpreter or legal assistance. The Committee is also concerned by the allegations of systematic violence and ill-treatments committed by the police forces towards asylum seekers. Read more in ENG
