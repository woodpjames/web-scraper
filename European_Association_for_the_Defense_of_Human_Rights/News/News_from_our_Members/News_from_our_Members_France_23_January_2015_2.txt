France: “Roma baby: how far will the exclusion go?”
23 January 2015
This post is also available in: 
    Français (French) In 2012, the Council of State established the right to accommodation as a fundamental freedom. Two years later, there have never been so many homeless people in France, having to sleep in a precarious accommodation or no accommodation at all. Whether they are longstanding homeless, precarious workers or unaccompanied foreign minors, the "115" (emergency shelters) or the hastily open gymnasiums struggle to accomodate them.
