State of emergency : freedoms under threat
2 May 2016
This post is also available in: 
    Français (French) 14 April 2016 – The Human Rights League of Luxembourg is extremely concerned with the consequences of the inclusion of the state of emergency in the constitution. If the Chamber and government insist anyway on doing so, the League wishes that considerably more effective guarantees are included in the text than those currently envisaged in order to avoid any risk of putting in danger the democratic system and citizens’ rights. You can read the whole paper of the Human Rights League of Luxembourg on the issue at: http://www.ldh.lu/ALOS-LDH-Communiqu%C3%A9_2016-04-14_%28%C3%A9tat_d’urgence%29.pdf . (in French only).
