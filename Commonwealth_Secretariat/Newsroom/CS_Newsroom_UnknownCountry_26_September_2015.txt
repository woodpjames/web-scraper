Ministerial Action Group deepens its work on democratic governance in Commonwealth countries
26 September 2015
New York, 25 September The nine foreign ministers who make up the Commonwealth Ministerial Action Group (CMAG) met at the United Nations today to discuss implementation of their enhanced mandate, enabling them to protect democratic governance and the rule of law more effectively. The enhanced mandate was adopted by Commonwealth Heads of Government in 2011. It lists eight circumstances that are violations of fundamental political values. If these circumstances occur in a Commonwealth member, they can trigger action by the group to help the country return to more democratic governance. At the New York meeting, ministers focused on two of the circumstances: The ministers issued a statement noting that limits on the independence of judges and constraints on political space undermine the culture of democracy in a country, and are contrary to the values in the Commonwealth Charter.
