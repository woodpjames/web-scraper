The killing of Pascal Kabungulu: 11 years on and still no justice
01.08.2016
Public statement
1 August 2016 – Prominent human rights defender Pascal Kabungulu was brutally murdered in 2005. On the eleventh anniversary of his death, Congolese civil society and international organisations demand that justice be finally delivered.
 
Pascal Kabungulu was a courageous and dedicated human rights defender, who worked on behalf of victims of human rights abuses in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC). He began receiving death threats after denouncing corruption in the mining sector, as well as extortion and ill-treatment of civilians in South Kivu.
On 31 July 2005, three armed men in military uniforms forced themselves into Pascal Kabungulu’s home, dragged him from his bedroom and shot him in front of his family. Though unanimously condemned inside and outside of the DRC, the circumstances of the murder were never elucidated and the culprits remain at large.
Following the murder, his wife, Déborah Kitumaini, also received death threats and was forced to flee the country with her six children. The family eventually resettled as refugees in Canada, where they now run the Pascal Kabungulu Foundation, which supports the families of human rights defenders assassinated in the DRC.
11 years without justice
A Commission of Enquiry was set up following the crime, but it dropped the case after preliminary examinations pointed at high-level suspects, including a former Governor of South Kivu and a high-ranking Colonel from the Congolese army. Three years later, a separate military tribunal also claimed it was incompetent to try these suspects. Neither the High Military Court nor the Supreme Court in Kinshasa have taken up the case, despite relentless lobbying from Congolese civil society.
With all domestic remedies seemingly blocked, Déborah Kitumaini and her children filed a complaint before the United Nations Human Rights Committee on 8 February 2016:
“Arresting and punishing the perpetrators will not bring back Pascal”, said Déborah Kitumaini. “Nevertheless, we are hoping that (…) justice is finally served, so that at the very least we can reclaim our lives without living in fear.”
A threat to human rights work throughout the country
Human rights defenders in eastern DRC operate in a highly dangerous context of armed conflict. Many are subjected to death threats, intimidation and harassment by state actors and armed groups. Others, like Pascal Kabungulu, have been killed for speaking out. In the vast majority of cases, these crimes remain shrouded by impunity.
“Through his work, Pascal Kabungulu touched the lives of thousands of Congolese men and women”, said Raphael Wakenge, Coordinator of the Congolese Initiative for Justice and Peace. “Failure to address impunity in this important case threatens human rights work throughout the entire country.”
 
We, the undersigned, call on the Congolese authorities and the international community to ensure justice is finally rendered for Pascal Kabungulu’s murder, and to take all measures necessary to protect human rights defenders in the DRC.
