Yasuji Okamura
11.02.2013
facts 

Yasuji Okamura was born in Tokyo in 1884, he attended Waseda University, Tokyo Junior Army School, and Army Central Junior School. Okamura entered the 16th class of the Imperial Japanese Army Academy in 1899 and graduated in 1904, together with future generals Itagaki Seishiro, Kenji Doihara and Ando Rikichi.
In 1910, Okamura entered the 25th class of the Army War College, and was promoted to captain in 1913. During and after World War I, he served in a number of staff positions on the Imperial Japanese Army General Staff. In the early 1920s, he moved briefly to China to serve as a military advisor to a Chinese warlord.
From 1932 to 1934, Okamura was Vice chief-of-staff of the Shanghai Expeditionary Army in the Kwangtung Army and served as military attaché to Manchukuo.
Okamura was promoted to lieutenant general in 1936, and assigned command of the 2nd Division of the Imperial Japanese Army.
In 1938, Okamura was appointed commander in chief of the Japanese Eleventh Army, which participated in numerous major engagements in the Second Sino-Japanese War, notably the Battles of Wuhan, Nanchang and Changsha, during which, according to some historians, he was authorized to use chemical weapons.
In April 1940, Okamura was promoted to the rank of full general and the following year he was appointed the commander-in-chief of the Northern China Area Army.
In December 1941, Okamura was authorized, through Imperial General Headquarters Order Number 575 to implement the Three Alls Policy in north China, against the Chinese Red Army. According to historian Mitsuyoshi Himeta, the scorched earth campaign was responsible for the deaths of “more than 2,7 million” Chinese civilians.
In 1944, Okamura led the massive and largely successful Operation Ichigo against airfields in southern China, while retaining personal command of the Japanese Sixth Area Army. Later, he was appointed the commander-in-chief of the China Expeditionary Army.
With the surrender of Japan on 15 August 1945, Okamura represented the Imperial Japanese Army in the China Burma India Theater (CBI) official surrender ceremony held at Nanjing on 9 September 1945.
