Emmanuel Nkunzuwimye
23.04.2016
facts

Emmanuel Nkunzuwimye is an ethnic Rwandan who acquired Belgian nationality.
Nkunzuwimye allegedly participated in the 1994 genocide in Rwanda, during which about 800.000 Rwandans, mainly Tutsis, were killed.
He allegedly was a member of the Interahamwe, a Hutu paramilitary organization, which carried out the Genocide acts against the Tutsis in 1994. The members killed thousands of people by machetes and clubs and installed road blocks at crucial points, where they killed Tutsis, who were trying to flee.
According to the Rwandan newspaper “New Times” Nkunzuwimye was an accomplice of Jean-Marie Vianney Mudahinyuka, who is currently serving a 19-year prison sentence in Rwanda.
On 23 March 2011 Nkunzuwimye was arrested along with Ernest Gakwaya in Belgium and charged with genocide, war crimes and crimes against humanity.
