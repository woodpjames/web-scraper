Jovan Tintor, aged 65, was a senior official with the Serb Democratic Party (SDS) during Ex-Yugoslavia war. He was Karadzicâ€™s advisor, the president of the SDS in the municipality of Vogosca in Bosnia-Herzegovina and the head of the crisis Committee in the area. In this capacity, Tintor allegedly participated in the crimes perpetrated in Vogosca municipality.
In May 1992, after the Bosnian independence referendum, armed soldiers arrived and set up barricades in the Vogosca municipality. From that moment on, Croats and Bosniaks inhabitants fled or were expelled from their houses. They were allegedly persecuted, illegally detained, tortured, killed as well as subjected to forced labor in detention camp.
