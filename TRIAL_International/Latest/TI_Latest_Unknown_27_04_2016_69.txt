Mladen Blagojevic
27.04.2016
facts

Mladen Blagojevic, a Bosnian Serb, was born on 22 March 1971 in Bratunac. He is said to have been a member of the Bratunac brigade of the Serbian military police and therefore worked alongside Zdravko Bozic, Zeljko Zaric and Zoran Zivanovic his co-accused.
In 1998, Mladen Blagojevic took refuge in the United States, in Phoenix, Arizona. He was deported in 2006 once the authorities had discovered that it was only by lying about his past that he had managed to receive a permanent residency permit.
According to the indictment, Mladen Blagojevic, Zdravko Bozic, Zeljko Zaric and Zoran Zivanovic guarded the sites where thousands of Muslim civilians were being detained. Indeed, between 2,000 and 3,000 Bosnian Muslims are said to have been detained in the Vuk Karadzic primary school in Bratunac. These civilians came from the Srebrenica enclave. It is claimed that Mladen Blagojevic, Zdravko Bozic, Zeljko Zaric and Zoran Zivanovic abused, beat and treated the detainees cruelly.
The indictment also alleges that Blagojevic and Bozic killed five Bosnian civilians in the school on the night of 13 and 14 July 1995.
Finally, it is claimed that Mladen Blagojevic, Zoran Zivanovic, Zdravko Bozic and Zeljko Zaric guarded the site of the Grbavci school, located in Orahovac in the Zvornik municipality, on 14 July 1995. Between 1,000 and 2,500 detained civilians are said to have been held there. It is also said that the accused mistreated the prisoners.
The United States handed over Mladen Blagojevic to the Bosnian authorities in 2006, and he was placed in detention on 15 November of the same year.
