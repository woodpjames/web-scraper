Sasa Hudak
12.04.2016
facts 

Sasa Hudak was a member of the Territorial Defense Forces of the Yugoslav Republics in the Vukovar region in Croatia. This military unit, together with the Yugoslav People’s Army, fought against the forces of the Republic of Croatia on behalf of the Serbian minority which was seeking to secede from the Republic of Croatia between August 1991 and May 1992.
During October 1991 and May 1992, Hudak allegedly, participated in the killing, torture and robbery of non-Serbs living in the village Miklusevci which had fallen to Serb forces.
Hudak has been accused of forcing 98 residents to leave the village on 18 May 1992, robbing them of their money, jewellery and other valuables by throwing bombs into the houses and threatening them with firearms. According to the allegations, four persons were killed and six persons were tortured and beaten. These acts were performed on behalf of the self-proclaimed government of the Serb Autonomous Oblast (SAO) of Krajina allegedly with the object of expelling the non-Serb population from their own homes to prevent and destroy their further existence.
On 29 April 1996 ,Sasa Hudak was charged with genocide together with 34 other people under Article 119 of the Basic Criminal Law of the Republic of Croatia by the Osijek County State’s Attorney Office.
