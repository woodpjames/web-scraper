Ilya Mashonkin
27.04.2016
facts

Ilya Mashonkin was born in 1915 and he was a former member of the NKVD secret police of the Soviet Union when he was stationed in Latvia after the Soviet army regained control of the country towards the end of World War II.
Mashonkin was accused of being involved in the deportation of 42.000 Latvians to work camps in Siberia in March 1949 by having signed more than 1.500 deportation orders. As a senior agent, he was authorized to take and approve decisions regarding deportations. Evidence exists that Mashonkin took part in the deportation of at least 100 families. Many deportees reportedly died in exile.
On 18 March 1998, Ilya Mashonkin was arrested in Riga, Latvia.
