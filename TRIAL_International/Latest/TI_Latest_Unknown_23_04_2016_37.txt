Bozidar Kuvelja
23.04.2016
facts

Bozidar Kuvelja was born on 29 September 1971 in Cajnic, Bosnia and Herzegovina. He is also known as “Hercegovac”.
During the war he was member of special police force of Jahorina Training Centre. He allegedly participated in shooting of Bosniaks in Srebrenica in July 1995, forced gathering and transfer of women, children and elderly, to the territory controlled by the Republika Srpska Army. Also, he is accused of killing 100 Bosniaks who survived the shootings. In the indictment Kuvelja is accused of gathering and searching Bosniaks’ houses from 12 to 14 July 1995 and of taking the residents of the houses to the UN base in Potocari.
Kuvelja is on trial for violating the law of Bosnia and Herzegovina, namely Articles 171 and 31 which define what activities constitute genocide. The indictment was issued on 9 March 2011. The preliminary hearing started on 7 April 2011 and the trial started in 9 May 2011.
