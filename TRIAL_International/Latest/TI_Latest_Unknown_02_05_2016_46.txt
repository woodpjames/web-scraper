Arthur Seyss-Inquart
02.05.2016
facts

Arthur Seyss-Inquart was born on 27 July 1892 in Moravia, which, at the time, was part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire. He was the son of a school teacher and studied law before taking part in the First World War during which he was seriously wounded.
He subsequently became a lawyer and began to pursue extreme right wing ideas. He entered politics and was a proponent of the Anschluss, meaning the attachment of Austria to Germany. In 1938, the Austrian Chancellor von Schuschnigg made him Minister of the Interior. He briefly held the position of Chancellor, before Austria was annexed to the German Reich in March 1938. He became Governor of this newly created region of the Reich (the Ostmark) after its annexation.
Under Nazi rule, Seyss-Inquart held various positions: in particular, he was a member of the Reichstag, the German parliament, and a General in the SS. Seyss-Inquart, became Minister without Portfolio in Hitlerâ€™s cabinet. He also served as deputy to the Governor General of Poland (Hans Frank) following the German occupation of this country. In May 1940, he was appointed to the post of Reich Commissioner to the Netherlands.
Seyss-Inquart was accused of having used his various official functions, and his personal influence to promote the accession to power of the Nazi conspirators in Austria and the consolidation of this power, in terms of the count of indictment No1. Seyss-Inquart was also accused of participating in political planning and preparations with a view to waging wars of aggression in terms of counts of indictment Nos 1 and 2.. In addition he was also accused of having authorised, directed and taken part in acts constituting war crimes, according to count No 3, and of crimes against humanity according to count No 4, due especially to the numerous crimes committed against people and property.
Arthur Seyss-Inquart was arrested at the end of the war in Hamburg.
