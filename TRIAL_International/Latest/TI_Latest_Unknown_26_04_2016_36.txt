Basclay Humberto Zapata Reyes
26.04.2016
facts

Basclay Humberto Zapata Reyes was born in 22 October 1946 in Chile. Son of Luis Humberto and Lila, and actually residing at the Punta de Peuco prison in Santiago, Chile, Zapata Reyes is a former Chilean soldier, agent of Augusto Pinochet’s secret police.
In 1965 he joined a regiment of the Chillán area where he achieved a high ranking. In 1974 he became a commissioner at the National Intelligence Directorate (DINA), where he joined the Falcon Group I, devoted to the suppression of the Revolutionary Left Movement (MIR). He worked with Osvaldo Romo Mena under the command of Miguel Krassnoff Marchenko. Married to agent Maria Teresa Osorio, also known as “Marisol” and “Maria Soledad” (who worked at Villa Grimaldi), he called himself the “Troglo” because he used to rape female prisoners. It was an aggressive and brutal torturer. In 1992, once achieved the rank of sergeant, he was assigned to Army Intelligence Directorate (DINE). He was also a teacher at the School of non-commissioned officers.
It was found responsible for several human rights violations. He is the perpetrator of the death of Eulogio del Carmen Fritz Monsalve and took part in the capture of Diana Aron Svigilisky. He was also identified as one of the people who threw the corpse of Lumi Videla at the Italian Embassy. Together with Romo Mena, he was found responsible for the disappearance of Alfonso Chanfreau Oyarce.
Following the arrest of Augusto Pinochet on 16 October 1998 in London, between October and November 1998 five Franco-Chilean families of victims of the Pinochet’s dictatorship filed complaints in France.
