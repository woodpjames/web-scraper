José Domingo Ríos Martínez
28.04.2016
facts

José Domingo Ríos Martínez was a military commissioner within the military area of Zacapa during the 36 years of civil war in Guatemala.
On 19 October 1981, Jacobo Crisóstomo Chegüen, Miguel Ángel Chegüen Crisóstomo, Raúl Chegüen, Inocente Gallardo, Antolín Gallardo Rivera, Valentín Gallardo Rivera, Santiago Gallardo Rivera and Tránsito Rivera in the village El Jute –all from the municipality and department of Chiquimula, were abducted by military personnel of the military area of Zacapa.
Marco Antonio Sanchez Samayoa was in command of the military Area of Zacapa and responsible for controlling the civilian population. Ríos Martínez, the day of the facts, was one of the military commissioners in El Jute, along with Gabriel Álvarez Ramos and Salomón Maldonado Ríos. The commissioners informed the commandant of the area that there was a guerrilla centre in that community. On 19 October 1981, these military commissioners accompanied the militaries to locate and abduct the eight aforementioned people.
The victims allegedly suffered cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment upon being abducted –while accusing them of being members of the guerrilla. They were transferred to the military area of Zacapa without making them available to the competent authority.
On 13 June 2005, warrant of arrest and preventive detention as well as indictment were issued against Marco Antonio Sánchez Samayoa, José Domingo Ríos Martínez and Gabriel Álvarez Ramos.
