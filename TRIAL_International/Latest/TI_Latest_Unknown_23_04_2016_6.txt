He was suspected of having joined paramilitary death squads, supported by the government, and of having participated in the torture and killing of members of the communist guerilla movement.
In 2002, he took refuge in the United Kingdom where he sought political asylum.
In the process of his application for asylum, Grande-Vargas gave very detailed accounts of his past and his involvement in the torture, as well as in the detention and killing of numerous persons in Peru. This information was passed to the counter terrorism department of the Metropolitan Police.
