Gerard Ntakirutimana
31.05.2016
facts

Gérard Ntakirutimana was born in 1957 in Ngoma, Rwanda. He was a doctor at the hospital of the Mugonero Complex, placed under the direction of the Association of the Seventh Day Adventists.
Between April and June 1994, Gérard Ntakirutimana allegedly instigated and participated in murders and infliction of serious bodily and mental harm on Tutsi civilians in the regions of Mugonero and Bisesero.
On 16 April 1994, he reportedly provided police officers ammunition for the attack on the Mugonero Adventist Complex, where civilian Tutsis had taken refuge. Several hundred men, women and children were killed during the attack. Gérard Ntakirutimana is reported to have himself killed one of these victims, Charles Ukobizaba.
Moreover, in the same period of 1994, Gérard Ntakirutimana allegedly took part in several attacks on civilians in the Bisesero region, which accounted for several deaths. He is said to have shot at refugees at Gitwe Hill, Mubuga Primary School, Mutiti Hill and Kidashya Hill, where he also transported attackers. On one occasion, he was reported to have slain a Tutsi civilian named Esdras during an attack on the Gitwe primary school.
