Robert Bales
18.04.2016
facts

Robert Bales is a former member of the US Armed Forces. He was born in Norwood, Ohio, as the youngest of five brothers. He studied economics at the Ohio University and later worked as a stockbroker before joining the army in November 2001.
After participating in various military units, he was sent to Iraq from 2003 to 2004, 2006 to 2007, and finally from 2009 to 2010. On 1 November 2008 Bales was promoted to the rank of the second sergeant of the American army.
On 1 February 2012 Bales was sent to Afghanistan, where he was stationed in the Belambai base, in the province of Kandahar. He was responsible for providing security and support for the Armed Special Forces as well as for Navy SEALs conducting stabilization operations in the area.
On 11 March 2012, as a result of events occurred in villages Balandi and Alkozai, located near the American military base of Belambai, 16 Afghan civilians, including four women and two children, were killed and six more injured. The investigation, conducted by members of the US military, concluded two weeks later that Bales had been responsible for the actions occurred. It was established that that night he had been drinking alcohol and after committing the first attack, Bales returned to the camp. After staying there for an hour, he came out and committed the second one. Once captured, he confessed to the crime.
