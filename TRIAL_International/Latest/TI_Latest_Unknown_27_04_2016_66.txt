Pascual Oscar Guerrieri
27.04.2016
facts

Pascual Oscar Guerrieri was born on 30 December 1934 in Buenos Aires. On completion of his secondary education at the end of the sixties, he went to Military College. In 1968 and 1969, he was trained as a technician in military intelligence at the Campo de Mayo. Following this he served in Battalion 601. In 1973 and 1974, he was stationed at a Military Intelligence unit in Rio Gallegos in Patagonia.
In December 1976, he was appointed to the Military Intelligence Corps 11, with the rank of Major and subsequently became second in command of section 121 in Rosario. On 24 August 1977, he was promoted to the rank of Lieutenant-colonel, becoming Commander of Detachment 121 with responsibility, until 1978, for the undercover detention centers of La Calamita, Quinta de Funes, Escuela Magnasco and La Intermedia de la region de Rosario.
On 26 January 1979, he was transferred to Comodoro Rivadavia, becoming commander of Military Intelligence unit 183. On 7 November 1980, he was again assigned to Battalion 601 heading up the Operations Center until November 1981, after which he returned to Intelligence Detachment 121 in Rosario. On 1 December 1981, he was promoted to the rank of Colonel.
With the return to democracy in 1983, he fled to Paraguay. Afterwards, taking advantage of the Due Obedience law he returned to Argentina. In 1987, he retired from the army with the rank of Colonel.
In 1989, he was employed by the State Intelligence Secretariat (SIDE, the Argentinean Secret Service) where he remained until 1999.
In 2002, Guerrieri was arrested and imprisoned in the Military Penitential Institute at Campo de Mayo, after being charged with crimes against humanity in the “Battalion 601” affair. In 2010 and 2011, he was put on trial for the affairs known as the “Parana Military Hospital” and the “Quinta de Funes Detention Center”.
