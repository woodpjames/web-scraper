Joseph Nzirorera
31.05.2016
facts

Joseph Nzirorera was born in 1950 in the commune of Mukingo in Rwanda. At the time of the events, he was the Secretary General of the National Republican Movement for Development and Democracy (MRND, the party of President Juvenal Habyarimana) in which position he exercised authority over the Interahamwe militia (an extremist Hutu militia). He was also involved in various political activities. Notably, in July 1994, he was President of the National Council for Development, the Rwandan Parliament
From the end of 1990 onwards, Joseph Nzirorera, in concert with several other members of the government in place in Rwanda, were said to have planned the extermination of the Tutsi population and the elimination of members of the opposition. In 1994 during the execution phase of this plan, Joseph Nzirorera and his colleagues were reported to have organised, ordered and participated in massacres against Rwandan civilians. In particular Joseph Nzirorera was said to have personally provided for training and weapons for the Interahamwe.
Joseph Nzirorera was arrested in Benin on 5 June 1998 and transferred to the International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR) in Arusha, on 17 July 1998.
