Peter Van Niekerk
19.04.2016
facts

Peter Van Niekerk is a South African citizen and was in charge of the security for the Anvil Mining company at its copper and silver mine in Dikulushi.
On 14 October 2004, the town of Kilwa came under the control of the Revolutionary Movement for the Liberation of Katanga. On 15 October, the town was retaken by the 62nd Brigade of the Armed Forces of the Democratic Republic of the Congo (FARDC) led by Colonel Ad√©mar Ilunga. During the operation, the FADRC were accused of having committed serious violations of human rights and of international humanitarian law. Ilunga and his men are alleged to have summarily executed at least 25 civilians, and to have indulged in rape, torture, illegal detention, looting and wanton destruction throughout the town. The United Nations Mission to Congo (MONUC) reported that more than 100 persons were killed
Peter Van Niekerk, together with two other employees of Anvil Mining, Cedric Kirsten and Pierre Mercier allegedly facilitated the commission of the crimes by providing logistical support to the 62nd Brigade.
Peter Van Niekerk was the only one of the three Anvil Mining employees indicted to appear on one occasion before the Lubumbashi Military High Court.
