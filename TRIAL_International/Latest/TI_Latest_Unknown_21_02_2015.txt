Augustin Ngirabatware
21.02.2015
facts 

Augustin Ngirabatware was born on 12 January 1957 in Nyamyumba, in Gisenyi prefecture, Rwanda. Doctor of Economics and Professor at the National University of Rwanda, he was General Director of the Minister of Mines and Artisanat, prior to his appointment as Minister of Planning on 9 September 1990.
Augustin Ngirabatware held the office of Minister of Planning with various governments of the National Republican Movement for Democracy and Development (MRND- French acronym) including those o f 15 January 1989, 9 July 1990, 4 February 1991, and as part of the first pluralist government of 31 December 1991, the second multi-party government of 16 April 1992, and the third multi-party government of 18 July 1993; and in the interim Government of 9 April 1994.
Due to his high academic standing, his ongoing government role from 1990 in a key, highly funded, Ministry and being the son-in-law of a very wealthy business man, Félicien Kabuga, (himself allied to President Juvénal Habyarimana), Augustin Ngirabatware was a very influential member of the MRND in Gisenyi Ngirabatware was charged with conspiracy to commit genocide during the period from the end of 1990 to July 1994, through the elaboration of a plan to exterminate the Tutsi civilian population, and to eliminate his political opponents in order to remain in power. The plan involved, among other things, the instigation of ethnic violence, the training and weapons supply to militias and the drawing up of lists of people to be eliminated. In the implementation of the plan, Ngirabatware was said to have organized, ordered and taken part in the massacres of civilian Tutsis and moderate Hutus.
In a letter dated 3 December 1993 addressed to the Commander of UNAMIR (United Nations Assistance Mission for Rwanda) as well as to Ngirabatware and other members of the Government, members of the Rwandese Armed Forces (RAF) revealed the existence of a project, conceived by extremist Hutu militaries. The aim of the project was to jeopardize the Arusha Agreements in order to remain in power, through the extermination of the Tutsi and their allies. The letter contained a list of political opponents to be eliminated. Some of them were actually executed in the morning of 7 April 1994.
Augustin Ngirabatware was indicted by the International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR) on 1 October 1999.
