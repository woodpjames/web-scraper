Enes Viteskic
18.04.2016
facts

Enes Viteskic was born on 24 September 1969 in Osijek, Croatia. He finished secondary school and from 1988 to 1989 Viteskic served in the Yugoslav National Army. He joined the Croatian Army in 1991 and became a member of the 1st Battalion, 2nd Infantry Company of the 130th Brigade. Viteskic was a resident of Vladislavaca, where he worked as a wood processor at the time of his indictment.
On 11 December 1991 Viteskic and Nikola Ivankovic, along with several other persons, carried out an alleged act of revenge in the village of Paulin Dvor, Croatia following the alleged killing of their comrade, Ilija Ravnjak, by Serbs. Viteskic and the others entered the home of Andrija Bukvic, where Serb members of the community were staying. Viteskic and the others attacked with automatic rifles and hand grenades. Nineteen civilians were allegedly killed in the attack. Most of the victims were elderly and nine were women. The house was blown up the next day.
The Croatian army allegedly transported the bodies of 18 of the victims to a warehouse in Lug under the control of the Croatian military where they were hidden until January 1997. The bodies were then buried in a mass grave in Rizvanusa. The bodies were found in Rizvanusa on 13 May 2002. The nineteenth victim was found in Paulin Dvor.
