Zoran Vukovic
27.04.2016
facts

Zoran Vukovic was born on 6 September 1955 in Brusna, in the Foca region of South East Bosnia. Before the conflict, he lived in Foca and worked as a waiter and as a chauffeur. Between April 1992 and February 1993, he was a (sub) commanding officer in the army of the Serbian Republic of Bosnia Herzegovina and a paramilitary leader in the Foca region.
In the summer of 1992, the Foca region fell under Serbian forces. The population of the city was approximately 40,000 inhabitants, half of whom were Muslim. The Serbian leadership separated men and women and detained thousands of Muslims and Croats in different centres. During the arrests, many civilians were killed, harmed or victim of sexual violence.
According to the indictment, Zoran Vukovic participated in the collective rape and mistreatment of many women, the youngest of which were barely teenagers.
On 23 December 1999, Zoran Vukovic was arrested by SFOR (The Stabilisation Force of Nato) and was rendered to the International Criminal Court for Former Yugoslavia (ICTY).
