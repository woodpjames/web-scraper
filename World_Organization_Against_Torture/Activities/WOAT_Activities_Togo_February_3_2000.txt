Togo:  Arrest of two trade-union leaders
February  3, 2000
Save as PDF Print versionHuman rights defenders / Urgent Interventions / Togo / 2000 / FebruaryTogo: Arrest of two trade-union leadersURGENT APPEAL -THE OBSERVATORY  TGO 001 / 0200 / OBS 004 Arrest  TOGO  3rd February 2000  The Observatory for the Protection of Human Rights Defenders, a joint programme of the FIDH and the OMCT, requests your intervention in the following situation in Togo.  Brief description of the situation  The Observatory has been informed by various sources, including the WFTU a member of the OMCT, of the arrest, on January 28th 2000 of two trade-union leaders, MM. Tetevi Norbert Gbikpi-Benissan, Secretary General of the  Union Nationale des Syndicats Indépendants du Togo (UNSIT), and Pierre Alaga-Kodegui, Secretary General of the Fédération des Travailleurs de l'Enseignement (FETREN).  According to information received these persons are allegedly accused of "propagating false information" and were immediately taken to the prison of Lomé.  The Observatory fears that the arrest of the above mentioned persons is due to their activities in defence of the fundamental rights and liberties of the workers.  It also fears that their physical safety and integrity may be seriously jeopardized.  Action requested : Please write to the Togolese authorities urging them to:  i.	guarantee the physical and psychological integrity of  MM. Tetevi Norbert Gbikpi-Benissan et Pierre Alaga-Kodegui; and ensure their immediate release in the absence of valid charges against them; ii.		adopt all necessary measures in order to ensure full freedom of action of human rights organizations including trade union organizations as well as their members; iii.	to conform to the arrangements of the Declaration of Human Rights Defenders adopted by the General Assembly of the United Nations on the 9 December 1998, and notably by Article 1 which states that « Everyone has the right, individually and in association with others, to promote and to strive for the protection and realization of human rights and fundamental freedoms at the national and international levels» and Article 6.b which stipulates that « Everyone has the right, individually and in association with others (...), to publish, impart or disseminate to others views, information and knowledge on all human rights and fundamental freedoms ».   iv.	to conform, more generally, to the dispositions of the universal Declaration of Human Rights and the international pacts relative to Human Rights which bind Togo.
