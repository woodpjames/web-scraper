Sudan:  Six individuals released however arbitrary detention continues
August  7, 2007
Save as PDF Print versionUrgent campaigns / Urgent Interventions / Sudan / 2007 / AugustSudan: Six individuals released however arbitrary detention continuesCase SDN 200607.1 Follow up of case SDN 200607 Release/ Whereabouts located/ Arbitrary arrest and detention/ Incommunicado detention/ Fear of torture and ill-treatment Geneva, 7 August 2007 The International Secretariat of the World Organisation Against Torture (OMCT) received new information on the following situation in Sudan. New information The International Secretariat of OMCT has been informed by the Sudan Organisation Against Torture (SOAT), a member of the SOS-Torture network, of the release of six individuals (including four journalists) and the whereabouts of three lawyers. However, it has also been informed of the abritrary arrest and detention of two other individuals following the Kajabar Dam protest. According to the information received, Mr. Abd Allah Abd Alghume (in urgent appeal (UA) SDN 200607 written Ali Abdelgayoum), Mr. Emad Merghni Seed Ahmed (in UA SDN 200607 written Imad Mirghani Sid-Ahmed) and Mr. Alam Aldeen Abd Alghni (in UA SDN 200607 written Alam Eldin Abdelgayoum), three lawyers who were arrested in Dongola on 13 June 2007, are all being detained in Dabak prison, North of Khartoum. Two additional individuals were reportedly arrested following the protest and are also currently being detained in Dabak prison:  Mr. Osman Shammat, a driver arrested in Dongola on 13 June 2007, and Dr. Mohammed Julal Hashim, a university lecturer arrested in Khartoum on 15 June 2007.  Both Mr. Alam Aldeen Abd Alghni and Dr. Mohammed Julal Hashim have been reportedly allowed a 15 minutes visit by relatives. The others have been refused visits from their families and lawyers. Regarding the four journalists Mr. Alfatih Abdullah (Al-Sudani daily), Mr. Qazafi Abdulmotalab (Al-Ayam daily), Mr. Abuobaida Awad (Rai-Alshaab daily) and Mr. Abulgasim Farahna who were arrested on 13 June 2007 in Dongola, they were reportedly released from Kober prison on 20 June 2007. According to the same information, Mr. Ahmed Rumrum, a 40 years old lawyer, was released on 16 June 2007 and Mr. Raafat Hasan Abass, a 40 years old private sector employee, was released on 23 June 2007. Both were arrested on 14 June 2007 in Khartoum. The International Secretariat of OMCT expresses its deep concern regarding the extended detention of the above mentioned individuals, especially the incommunicado detention of Mr. Osman Shammat, Mr. Abd Allah Abd Alghume and Mr. Emad Merghni Seed Ahmed. OMCT urges the Sudanese authorities to guarantee their physical and psychological integrity at all times and calls on their immediate release in the absence of valid legal charges, and if such charges exist, ensure that they are given a prompt and fair trial, in which their procedural rights are guaranteed at all times. Reminder of the situation According to the information received, on 13 June 2007 four persons were killed and nine other civilians were injured by the police and security forces in the Farraig village during a peaceful demonstration against the building of the Kajabar Dam in the area. According to the information, the four civilians who were killed are:  Mr. Shaik Aldin Haj Ahmed (m), 25 years old, Farmer Mr.  Mohamed Fagir Diab (m), 21 years old, High School student at Farraig Mr. Alsadig Salim (m), 40 years old, Farmer Mr. Almoiz Mohamed Abdelrahim(m), 21 years old  The injured persons are:  Mr. Sid Ahmed Osman Nori(m),  Miskaila village Mr. Khairi Osman Ismail, Miskaila village   Mr. Farag Abdelrahim Farag (m), Fad East Village Mr. Hamid Hamad Hamid (m), Kajabar Mr. Mursi Sid Ahmed (m),  Kajabar   Mr. Mohamed Mustafa (m), Jeddi village Mr. Osman Ibrahim(m), Farraig village Mr. Abdelrazig Yaqoub (m),  Miskaila village Mr. Alfadil Mohamed Fagiri (m),  Kajabar  The injured civilians were reportedly admitted later to Dongola hospital for medical treatment. Following this event, the security and intelligence forces in Dongola reportedly arrested and detained four journalists namely, Mr. Alfatih Abdullah (Al-Sudani daily), Mr. Qazafi Abdulmotalab (Al-Ayam daily), Mr. Abuobaida Awad (Rai-Alshaab daily) and Mr. Abulgasim Farahna (Alwan daily) and three lawyers namely Mr. Imad Mirghani Sid-Ahmed, Mr. Alam Eldin Abdelgayoum and Mr. Ali Abdelgayoum, on the late evening of 13 June 2007 at around 23:00, while they were passing by Dongola on their way to Kajabar dam area. According to the same information, on 14 June 2007, the security forces in Khartoum detained a further five people, in connection with the same incident of Kajabar dam demonstrations. The Nubians in Khartoum were having a protest demonstration against the killing of their people in Northern Sudan. Security forces reportedly raided the house of a Nubian family, at mid-night on 14 June 2007, searching for a Nubian activist who had spoken on the same day to the BBC radio on the issue of Kajabar killings. He was not there at the time they raided the house but they reportedly arrested five persons present in the house. They detained them and took them into a security house at St. 57 Amarat – Khartoum, where they were subjected to interrogation and investigation on their relation to the Nubian Movement and the Anti- Kajabar Dam Committee. Among the five detainees was reportedly SOAT’s lawyers network member Mr. Yasir Mohamed Salih (42 years old- lawyer) who happened to be in the house that night. Mr. Yasir Mohamed Salih and other two detainees, namely Mr. Abdulkareem Makki Abdulkareem, a 22 years old student and Mr. Faisal Abdulrahman Nasr, a 48 years old private sector employee, were released on 15 June 2007 at 6:00 am. Two others persons remained detained namely Mr. Ahmed Rumrum, a 40 years old lawyer and Mr. Raafat Hasan Abass, a 40 years old private sector employee. Requested Action Please write to the authorities in Sudan urging them to:  Guarantee, in all circumstances, the physical and psychological integrity of Mr. Abd Allah Abd Alghume, Mr. Emad Merghni Seed Ahmed, Mr. Alam Aldeen Abd Alghni, Mr. Osman Shammat, and Dr. Mohammed Julal Hashim; Guarantee access to their families and lawyers as well as guarantee any necessary medical assistance; Order their immediate release in the absence of valid legal charges, and if such charges exist, ensure that they are given a prompt and fair trial, in which their procedural rights are guaranteed at all times; Order a thorough and impartial investigation into these events, notably in the excessive use of force by security officers in Farraig, in order to identify all those responsible, bring them to trial and apply the penal and/or administrative sanctions as provided by law; Guarantee that adequate compensation is awarded to the victims and their families; Ensure the respect of human rights and fundamental freedoms throughout the country in accordance with national laws and international human rights standards.
