67th
Session of the UN General Assembly 
The Special Procedures Branch of OHCHR in
cooperation with the World Organisation Against Torture (OMCT), Penal Reform
International (PRI), the Center for Constitutional Rights (CCR) and Human
Rights Watch (HRW) cordially invite you to a side event on 
“THe
death penalty and human rights: the way forward”
A
review of the reports to the General Assembly of the Special Rapporteur on extrajudicial,
summary or arbitrary executions and the Special Rapporteur on torture and other
cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment or punishment
Wednesday,
24th October 2012
6:15 pm
– 7:45 pm
Conference
Room E, North Lawn Building (NLB)
New York

The UN Special Rapporteur on
extrajudicial, summary or arbitrary executions, Mr Christof Heyns, and the
Special Rapporteur on torture and other cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment
or punishment, Mr Juan Mendez, both will present their respective reports on
the death penalty to the General Assembly’s 67th Session. This
side-event shall intensify the impact of their thematic reports and further
generate the discussion around capital punishment in light of the global trend
towards abolition and possible new developments in law and practice. 
Speakers: 
- Mr Christof Heyns, UN Special Rapporteur on extrajudicial, summary or
arbitrary executions; 
- Mr Juan E. Méndez, UN Special Rapporteur on torture and other cruel,
inhuman or degrading treatment or punishment; 
- Mr Santiago Canton, Member of the Executive Council, World
Organisation Against Torture (OMCT); 
- Mrs Andrea Huber, Policy Director, Penal Reform International (PRI);
- Mrs Widney Brown, Senior Director for Law and Policy, Amnesty
International (AI);
- Mr Robert Renny Cushing, Executive Director,Murder Victims' Families for Human
Rights (MVFHR).
Moderator: 
- Maria McFarland, Acting Director, US Program, Human Rights Watch
(HRW).
UN identification badges are
required for entry into the North Lawn Building (NLB). Interested guests
without a UN identification badge should send an email to ak@omct.org
before 12pm (New York time) on Tuesday, 23rd October 2012.
