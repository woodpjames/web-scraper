Closing Guantánamo Will Require More Than the Stroke of a Pen
November 18, 2008
Amid the current debate about how the next U.S.  president should handle prisoners being held at Guantanamo Bay, Human Rights First Executive Director/CEO Elisa Massimino argues that:Closing Guantánamo will require more than the stroke of a pen. It will take comprehensive policy changes and a major investment of domestic and international political capital. But it can be done, and it can be done in the new administration's first year.Read her op-ed in the International Herald Tribune (11/18/08).
