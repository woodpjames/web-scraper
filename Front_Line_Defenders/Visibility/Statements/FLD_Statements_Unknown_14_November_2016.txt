International support for the creation of the International Advisory Group of Experts to investigate the murder of Berta Cáceres
14 November 2016
 We, the undersigned organisations, support the creation of the International Advisory Group of Experts (GAIPE), which was created to collaborate in the investigation of the murder of acclaimed indigenous rights defender, Berta Cáceres Flores, and the attempted murder of human rights defender Gustavo Castro Soto. Berta Cáceres Flores was the leader and coordinator of the Civic Council of Popular and Indigenous Organisations of Honduras (COPINH). Since the human rights defender was brutally murdered on 2 March 2016, we have accompanied Berta's family, COPINH and Honduran civil society in their struggle to seek truth and justice. We have called on Honduran authorities to carry out an independent and throughout investigation to ensure that the killers, as well as those that ordered her murder, are identified, prosecuted and punished for their crimes. However, despite numerous requests from national and international actors, Honduran authorities have remained silent and have not engaged with Berta's family members. We are concerned that so far the investigation has not made any progress in identifying those who ordered the murder of Berta Cáceres Flores, or in recognising the level of responsibility of those who have been arrested and charged in relation to the murder. With these facts in mind, the participation of GAIPE, an advisory group made up of independent investigators, is an important initiative that will allow for an objective contribution to the investigation process, and ensure that a rigorous investigation is undertaken, in accordance with international standards. It is worth noting that the participation of international human rights experts in internal processes has been key for bringing about structural changes in various countries and contexts. In the case of Honduras, the undersigned organisations hope that GAIPE will also provide recommendations, in order to overcome the root causes behind impunity in crimes against human rights defenders. We also ask Honduran Authorities to take all necessary measures to guarantee the security of the international human rights experts, so that the members and collaborators of GAIPE can carry out their activities without fear of interference. Finally, we urge the Honduran authorities to be cooperative and transparent in their collaboratation with GAIPE as this will ensure legitimacy in the investigation process and restore confidence in the justice system for the family of Berta Cáceres Flores, Honduran society, as well as for the international community.
