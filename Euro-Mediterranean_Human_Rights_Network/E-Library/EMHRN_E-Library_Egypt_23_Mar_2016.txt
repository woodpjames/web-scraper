Egypt: Unprecedented Crackdown on NGOs
23 Mar 2016
Economic & Social Rights,
Egypt,
Press Release,
Shrinking Space for Civil Society March 23, 2016 In recent weeks, the Egyptian authorities have summoned human rights workers for questioning, banned them from travel and attempted to freeze their personal funds and family assets. These steps indicate that a five-year-old investigation into the funding and registration of independent human rights groups could soon result in criminal charges, 14 international organisations said today. The authorities should halt their persecution of these groups and drop the investigation, which could threaten human rights defenders with up to 25 years in prison, the organizations said.
