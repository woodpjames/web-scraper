9 Apr 2018
Algeria,
Migration and Asylum April 09, 2018 Read in: 
French Today 9 April, the 28th session of the Committee on the Protection of the Rights of All Migrant Workers and Members of their Families is being held in Geneva, and Algeria is on the agenda. On this occasion, our member,  the National Autonomous Union of Public Administration Staff (SNAPAP), shares a new report on the situation of migrant workers. It is very clear that few improvements have been observed in Algeria since last year’s report. Kaddour Chouicha, a university professor, trade unionist and human rights activist in Algeria, outlines four main points to the Committee. Find here his intervention (French only).
